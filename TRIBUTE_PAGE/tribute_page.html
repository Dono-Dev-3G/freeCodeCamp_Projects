Ronnie "The King" Coleman

The 8x Mr. Olympia who only lifted light weights

Here's a chronology of Big Ron's bodybuilding feats:

  • 1990-1991 - First place at the 1990 Mr. Texas in heavyweight and overall categories, 3rd place at the 1990 NPC Nationals heavyweight division.

    1991 he turned pro with a 4th place at the NPC Nationals heavyweight and 1st place at the IFBB World Amateur Championships heavyweight division.

  • 1992-1994 - first participation at the Mr. Olympia contest: 1992 he ended up last (16th place). 14th at the Night of Champions and 11th at the Chicago Pro Championships.

    In 1993 6th place at the Chicago Pro Championships, 4th place at the IFBB French Grand Prix, 6th place at the IFBB German Grand Prix and 6th place in the Niagara Falls Pro.

    In 1994 Mr. Olympia Coleman ended up 15th. 3rd place at the IFBB French Grand Prix, 3rd place at the IFBB German Grand Prix and 4th place at the San Jose Pro Invitational.

  • 1995-1997 - In 1995 Coleman won his first pro show, winning the Canada Pro Cup. He also improved on his placement at the Mr. Olympia, getting 11th. 4th place at the IFBB French Grand Prix, 6th place at the IFBB Russian Grand Prix, 3rd place at the IFBB Ukrainian Grand Prix, 6th place at the Houston Pro Championships and 3rd place at the Night of Champions.

    In 1996 he got 6th at the Mr. Olympia. He won the Canada Pro Cup, secured 2nd place at the Florida Pro Invitational, 5th place at the IFBB English Grand Prix, 5th place at the IFBB German Grand Prix, 5th place at the IFBB Spanish Grand Prix and 2nd place at the Night of Champions.

    In 1997 He won the IFBB Russian Grand Prix, came 3rd at the Ironman Pro Invitational and the IFBB Finnish Grand Prix. At the Mr. Olympia in Long Beach, California he ended up 9th place. He also secured 4th at the Arnold Classic and the IFBB Czech Grand Prix, 5th at the IFBB English Grand Prix and the IFBB German Grand Prix, 6th at the IFBB Hungarian Grand Prix and the San Jose Pro Invitational, and 7th at the IFBB Spanish Grand Prix.

  • 1998 - Won his first Mr. Olympia title. 1st place at the IFBB Finnish Grand Prix, IFBB German Grand Prix, Toronto Pro and Night of Champions. Won his first Mr. Olympia.

  • 1999 - Won his second Mr. Olympia title. 1st place at the IFBB English Grand Prix and the World Pro Championships.

  • 2000 - Won his third Mr. Olymia title. 1st place at the IFBB English Grand Prix and 2000 World Pro Championships.

  • 2001 - Won his fourth Mr. Olympia title. 1st place at the Arnold Classic and IFBB New Zealand Grand Prix.

  • 2002 - Won his fifth Mr. Olympia title. 1st place at the IFBB Dutch Grand Prix and 2nd place at the Show of Strength Pro Championships.

  • 2003 - Won his 6th Mr. Olympia title. 1st place IFBB Russian Grand Prix.

  • 2004 - Won his 7th Mr. Olympia title. 1st place at the IFBB English Grand Prix, IFBB Dutch Grand Prix and IFBB Russian Grand Prix.

  • 2005 - Won his eighth Mr. Olympia title.

  • 2006-2007 - In 2006 his Mr. Olympia reign came to an end when he ended up 2nd place.

    In 2007 at his last Mr. Olympia he placed 4th.
